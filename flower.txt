California Poppy
Four thin bright orange petals slightly fading into yellow at the ends. Petals form a slight cup shape opening toward the sun. Found on the West Coast and Southwest United States. Grows in poor, sandy, well-drained soil and full sun. Easy to grow and seeds itself.

Yarrow
Feathery, fern-like green to gray-green foliage with white flowers blooming from May to June. Ideal for pollinator gardens. Durable and drought tolerant. Good for erosion control. Uses less water and can be a high-quality lawn substitute.
yarrow.jpeg

Sunflower
Bright yellow petals with a brown, wide flower head center. A perennial flower that can blossom year-round. Can withstand very dry soil. Its bright yellow color attracts native pollinators.
sunflower.jpeg

Blue Flax
A true blue flower, which is rare in nature. It has five fragile petals, and only one flower opens at a time on each stem. Eco-friendly for California landscapes. Drought tolerant, uses less water, provides resources for local pollinators, and helps with erosion control.
blueFlax.jpeg

Chuparosa
A multi-stemmed shrub with many red flowers. Environmentally friendly and drought tolerant. Supports the local ecosystem by attracting hummingbirds and butterflies with its nectar-rich flowers. Helps with erosion control and water conservation.
chuparosa.jpeg

Arroyo Lupine
Upright-growing flowers with purple-blue spike-like petals that bloom in spring and summer. Part of the legume family, which helps fix nitrogen into the soil. Important larval food for butterflies and moths. Grows well in both low- and high-fertility soils.
arroyoLupine.jpeg

Winecup Clarkia
Light pink or lavender flower with four thin, paper-like petals. Usually has a dark wine-colored center with streaks in the middle of the petals. Its cupped shape attracts pollinators. Found in all habitat zones in California except deserts. Grows to about one foot tall, has small 1/4-inch flowers, and is an annual plant.
